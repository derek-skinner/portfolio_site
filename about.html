Derek Skinner

Designer/Developer

Hello!

I am a designer living in the San Francisco Bay Area. I currently work at CBS Interactive and do freelance work in my spare time. Most of my days are spent designing in Photoshop/Sketch/Illustrator and coding HTML/CSS/JS. I am a big fan of the Sass preprocessor and prefer to code using that instead of regular CSS. My work is seen by millions of people every month on the web, at tech events such as CES, ComicCon, E3, Mobile World Congress, and even in Times Square, which is really humbling.

I started designing and teaching myself to code when I was in my teens and absolutely fell in love with it. I then decided to attend Expression College for Digital Arts where I earned my Bachelors degree in design. While attending college I started my freelance career where I worked for companies like Lux Design, the National Parks Service, and many smaller companies. Then about a year after I graduated college, CBS Interactive approached me and I have been working there ever since.

More recently, I have been focusing on contributing to and creating open source projects. I have released a few of my own open source projects which are aimed to help designers/developers. They are Sassy Swatches - a Sass color library, Blender - a Sass mixin collection, and Inflame Boilerplate, which is a grunt boilerplate that helps kickstart web projects.

Feel free to email me and say hi.
